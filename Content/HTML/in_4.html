SISTER MARY KENNETH KELLER

Sister Mary was born in Ohio in 1914. She entered the Sisters of Charity in 1932 and professed her vows in 1940.

EDUCATION

She went to DePaul University, where she received a B.S. in Mathematics and an M.S. in Mathematics and Physics. In 1965, she received her Ph.D. in Computer Science from The University of Wisconsin.

THESIS

Her thesis, "Inductive Inference on Computer Generated Patterns.", was written in CDC FORTRAN 63. In 1965, she became the first American female to obtain a Ph.D. in Computer Science.

CLARKE COLLEGE COMPUTER SCIENCE

She founded the computer science department at Clarke College in Iowa, which she directed for 20 years. She was passionate about providing access and information to everyone , not just computer scientists, to encourage independent thinking.
