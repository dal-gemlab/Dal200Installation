AWARD

She was named one of Canada’s 100 Most Powerful Women, the first woman from Nova Scotia to receive that honor in Arts and Communications. She was also noted as one of Canada’s top 5 Women of Inspiration by the Canadian Immigrant Magazine, and a Woman of Excellence by the Canadian Progress Club. She is also recognized as a digital diversity leader by Digital Nova Scotia and earned the 2017 award for Women Leaders in the Digital Economy.

CAREER

After she completed her BSc in Computer Science at Dalhousie University, she earned MA degree in Broadcasting Design and Motion Graphis. Since 2011, she has been a Principal and Creative Officer at WeUsTeam. WeUsThem is an internationally recognized full-service agency.

CONTRIBUTION

She is a director on the board for the Canadian Cancer Society and a Governor for the board of NSCAD University and the Halifax Chamber of Commerce. She also mentors the Women’s Executive Network, the Women’s Employment Outreach (WEO), the Halifax Partnership Connector Program, and acts as a Business Advisor with e3 (formerly Entrepreneurs Forum).

WiTs

She also helps the Women in Technology Society (WiTS) at Dalhousie. She provides young women with knowledge and advice about how to move forward in their careers after graduation.
