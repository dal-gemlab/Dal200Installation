COMPUTER SCIENCE

Julie completed a BEng in electrical engineering in 1986 (McGill), and an MASc in computer engineering in 1990 (Toronto). Her thesis focused on computational linguistics, a field of artificial intelligence.

CAREER IN COMPUTING

She worked as a systems engineer for IBM Canada's Science Engineering division in a high-performance computer architecture project. In 1991, she joined the Communications and Science Department of the IBM Zurich Research Laboratory in Switzerland. In 1992, she joined Bell-Northern Research in Montreal where she led a project in telephone speech comprehension using computer voice recognition.

NASA AND NATO

From 1992 to 2013, She worked as an astronaut and flew two missions in space. She served as a technical advisor for the Mobile Servicing System, an advanced robotics system and Canada's contribution to the International Space Station. In 1993, she built the Human-Computer Interaction Group at the Canadian Astronaut Program and worked in the NATO International Research Study Group on speech processing.

GOVERNER GENERAL

Julie was announced on July 13, 2017, as Prime Minister Justin Trudeau's recommendation to be the 29th Governor General of Canada.
