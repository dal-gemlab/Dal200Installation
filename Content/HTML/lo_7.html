STUDIES

Before moving to Canada, Rita received a MSc in Information Systems from Middle East Technical University, Turkey, and a BSc in Computer Science from Nnamdi Azikiwe University, Nigeria. She received her PhD in Computer Science from the University of Saskatchewan in 2014. She recently joined Dalhousie from the University of Waterloo.

BANTING POSTDOCTORAL FELLOW

She was a Banting Postdoctoral Fellowship (NSERC) researcher at the University of Waterloo. She was also a Postdoctoral Researcher with ACT Lab, investigating how interactive technologies can be designed to motivate desirable behaviors in older adults. Before joining McGill University in 2015, she was a Visiting Senior Lecturer at Nnamdi Azikiwe University, Nigeria, and a Postdoctoral Fellow at Yale University.

AWARDS

She was chosen in recognition as one of the 150 Canadian Women In Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) in 2017. She is dedicated to helping inspire the next generation of female tech leaders. She was also a recipient of the prestigious Vanier Scholarship for her Ph.D. research on Developing Persuasive Technology for Health at the University of Saskatchewan.

RESEARCH

She is a researcher in Human-Computer Interaction and has produced more than 50 peer reviewed papers in only five years, along with taking part in over 20 media appearances, and receiving more than 500 Google Scholar citations.
